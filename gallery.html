Featured Moments

Highlights from our most impactful programs and events

Community Mural Project - Kampala

Over 50 young artists came together to create a powerful 30-meter mural addressing HIV stigma. This permanent installation serves as an ongoing educational tool for the Kamwokya community.

Community Art HIV Awareness

Kampala • August 2023

Mental Health Poetry Night - Gulu

A groundbreaking event where 200+ youth shared their mental health stories through spoken word. The evening created safe spaces for healing conversations in post-conflict communities.

Mental Health Spoken Word

Gulu • March 2024

Youth Film Premiere - Mbarara

"Breaking Chains" - our youth-produced short film about HIV stigma - premiered to over 300 community members. The film sparked critical conversations and policy discussions at the district level.

Film Production Community Engagement

Mbarara • November 2023

Our Work Across Uganda

Tailored programming creating impact in each community we serve

Kampala

Urban youth engagement through digital arts, performance workshops, and community murals addressing HIV awareness and mental health.

15+ community events • 400+ youth engaged

Gulu

Post-conflict mental health healing through creative therapies, community art projects, and trauma-informed performance programs.

12+ healing circles • 250+ participants

Kotido

Culturally-sensitive health education programs designed specifically for pastoral communities, incorporating traditional art forms.

8+ cultural workshops • 180+ community members

Luuka

Rural SRHR education through community theater, traditional dance, and interactive mural projects engaging entire villages.

10+ village programs • 200+ youth reached

Arua

Border community health initiatives addressing cross-border challenges through multilingual arts programs and mobile outreach.

6+ border programs • 120+ youth engaged

Mbarara

University-focused creative health education, peer-to-peer programs, and student-led advocacy initiatives reaching higher education institutions.

8+ campus events • 150+ students involved

Community Impact Stories

Real stories of transformation from the communities we serve

Sarah's Story - From Stigma to Strength

"Before joining Elpis, I was afraid to speak about my HIV status. Through art therapy and community support, I found my voice. Now I lead workshops helping other young women break free from stigma."

Sarah, 21 • Kampala Program Participant & Peer Educator

David's Journey - Healing Through Art

"Growing up in post-conflict Gulu, I carried trauma that I couldn't express. Elpis taught me that art could be medicine. Today, I facilitate healing circles and help others transform pain into purpose."

David, 24 • Community Art Therapist & Program Alumni

Community Leader Perspective

"Elpis didn't just bring programs to our community - they brought hope. The murals our youth created are daily reminders that health challenges don't define us. They inspire us to keep fighting stigma."

Margaret Akello • LC3 Chairperson, Kamwokya
