What We Do

Innovative, arts-based solutions addressing HIV/AIDS, mental health, and gender equality

1. Advocacy Through Artistry

We harness the power of creativity to challenge misconceptions and create meaningful dialogue around health issues.

Youth Advocate Training

Training young advocates to use creative mediums as tools for health education and stigma reduction.

Award-Winning Films

Creating powerful short films that challenge misconceptions about HIV/AIDS and mental health.

Nationwide Exhibitions

Transforming personal stories into public dialogue through art exhibitions and installations.

Digital Engagement

Hosting viral X-Space discussions that reach thousands of young people across Uganda.

2. Community Outreach

Tailored programs across six strategic regions, each addressing unique local needs and cultural contexts.

Kampala

Engaging urban youth through digital and performing arts, addressing the unique challenges of city life and modern relationships.

Gulu

Post-conflict mental health support through creative therapies, helping communities heal and rebuild resilience.

Kotido

Culturally-sensitive programs for pastoral communities, respecting traditional values while promoting health awareness.

Luuka

Rural SRHR education through murals and theater, making health information accessible and engaging for rural youth.

Arua

Border community health initiatives, addressing cross-border health challenges and refugee community needs.

Mbarara

University-focused creative health education, targeting higher education institutions and young professionals.

3. Strategic Partnerships

Building powerful alliances to amplify our impact and create sustainable change.

10 Million Hearts Campaign

Large-scale advocacy campaign reaching millions of Ugandans with health messages and support resources.

School Collaborations

Working directly with educational institutions to integrate health education into school programs.

Corporate Partnerships

Engaging private sector partners to support youth health initiatives and provide sustainable funding.

Government Alliances

Collaborating with government agencies to align our programs with national health priorities and policies.

Our Approach

Youth-Centered

Young people lead our programs, ensuring relevance and authenticity in our messaging and methods.

Arts-Based

We use creativity as a universal language to break down barriers and create emotional connections.

Culturally Sensitive

Our programs respect local cultures while promoting progressive health attitudes and behaviors.

Evidence-Informed

We base our strategies on research and continuously evaluate our impact to improve effectiveness.
